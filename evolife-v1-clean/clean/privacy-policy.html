FRIDAYS PRIVACY NOTICE

Effective Date: October 11, 2023

Last Updated: June 10, 2025

This Privacy Notice describes how Thrive Health Group, Inc. (“Fridays”) uses, discloses, and protects the personal information we collect through the Fridays website, JoinFridays.com.

Key Privacy Points

We hope you read this entire Privacy Notice for full detail, but here are some key points:

  • Fridays does not presently provide medical advice.
  • Instead, we help individuals establish and maintain a relationship with certain third-party healthcare providers (“Healthcare Providers”).  We also provide some non-medical services and information to our users.
  • We ask new users some initial qualifying questions about themselves and their health. These questions were approved by the relevant Healthcare Providers.  If the answers to those questions meet certain criteria specified by a Healthcare Provider, the Healthcare Provider will invite the user to schedule an initial appointment and establish an account on the Healthcare Provider’s own patient portal (the “Patient Portal”), which is not operated by Fridays or Fridays’ service providers.  The Patient Portal may have the Fridays logo, but it is operated by the Healthcare Provider or its service providers.
  • At the Patient Portal, the user will answer a more detailed questionnaire designed by the Healthcare Provider, which will request additional medical information and other personal information.  
  • Payment, diagnosis, and any provision of telehealth will happen through the Patient Portal and other services operated by the Healthcare Provider or its service providers (collectively with the Patient Portal, the “Healthcare Provider Sites”), not through Fridays’ site.  Diagnosis or treatment may also require obtaining lab work or prescriptions through third-party labs and pharmacies that are not operated by or for Fridays (collectively, “Third Party Suppliers”).
  • The user’s relationship with the Healthcare Provider, and the user’s use of the Patient Portal and other Healthcare Provider Sites, are subject to the privacy policies and practices of the Healthcare Provider and its service providers, not this Privacy Notice, even though the Fridays logo may appear on the Patient Portal.  
  • The user’s relationship with Third Party Suppliers is subject to the privacy policies and practices of the Third Party Suppliers and their service providers, not this Privacy Notice (regardless of whether the Fridays logo appears as part of the user’s interactions with them).

  1. Information We Collect on JoinFridays.com with Cookies and Other Technology

Through JoinFridays.com, we and third parties may collect information from your computer or other device by automated means such as cookies, web beacons, local storage, JavaScript, mobile-device functionality and other computer code. This information may include unique browser identifiers, IP address, browser and operating system information, device identifiers (such as advertising identifiers), other device information, Internet connection information, as well as details about your interactions with JoinFridays.com (for example, the URL of the third-party website from which you came, the pages on our website that you visit, and the links you click on in a website). In some cases (such as certain cookies), the tools described here involve storing unique identifiers or other information on your device for later use.

‍What Are Cookies?  Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, smartphone, etc.) when you visit a website. They help the website recognize your device and store information about your preferences or past actions.

‍Types of Cookies We May Use  

‍1. Necessary Cookies: These cookies are essential for the operation of our website. They enable you to navigate the site and use its features, such as accessing secure areas.

2. Performance Cookies: These cookies collect information about how visitors use our website, such as which pages are visited most often. This helps us improve the way our website works.

3. Functionality Cookies: These cookies allow our website to remember choices you make (such as your username, language, or the region you are in) and provide enhanced, more personalized features.

4. Targeting/Advertising Cookies: These cookies are used to deliver advertisements more relevant to you and your interests. They are also used to limit the number of times you see an ad and to help measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns.

‍How to Manage Cookies  Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings preferences. However, if you choose to disable cookies, it may affect the functionality and features of our website. To learn more about cookies and how to manage or delete them, visit www.allaboutcookies.org.

‍Third-Party Cookies  We may also use third-party cookies from service providers to help us analyze website usage, track our marketing efforts, and deliver advertisements. These third-party cookies are subject to the respective privacy policies of the third-party providers.

‍Changes to This Cookies Notice We may update this Cookies Notice from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. Please revisit this Cookies Notice regularly to stay informed about our use of cookies.

‍Fridays Consumer Health Data Privacy Policy

‍Last Updated: July 17, 2024

This Consumer Health Data Privacy Policy supplements the Fridays Privacy Notice (the “Privacy Notice”) and applies to personal data defined as “consumer health data” (“CHD”) by the Washington state My Health My Data Act (“MHMDA”) and Nevada’s Consumer Health Data Privacy Law (“Nevada CHD Law”). Undefined capitalized terms have the meaning in the Privacy Notice.  Much of our processing of CHD is subject to the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (“HIPAA”) and thus is exempt from the MHMDA and the Nevada CHD Law.  However, in the spirit of transparency, this Privacy Policy also describes some of our HIPAA-regulated activities and other activities for which these state laws do not require us to provide a privacy policy.

‍Categories of CHD Collected

‍As described further in our Privacy Notice, and depending on applicable law and how you interact with Fridays, we may collect the following categories of CHD, as broadly defined in the MHMDA and Nevada CHD Law:

  • Individual health conditions, treatment, diseases, or diagnoses: E.g. in connection with you seeking healthcare services or medications through Fridays.
  • Social, psychological, behavioral, and medical interventions: E.g. through collection of your medical history as part of the treatment services you seek through Fridays.
  • Health-related surgeries or procedures: E.g. through collection of health-related surgeries or procedures within your medical history as part of the treatment services you seek through Fridays.
  • Use or purchase of prescribed medication: E.g. through your purchase of medication through Fridays or through the medical history you provide.
  • Bodily functions, vital signs, symptoms, or measurements of health information: E.g. as part of you seeking healthcare services or medications through Fridays.
  • Diagnoses or diagnostic testing, treatment, or medication: E.g. as part of the medical history collected by Fridays, or when you otherwise seek healthcare services or medications through Fridays.
  • Gender-affirming care information: E.g. as part of the medical history collected by Fridays, or when you otherwise seek healthcare services or medications through Fridays.
  • Reproductive or sexual health information: E.g. as part of the medical history collected by Fridays, or when you otherwise seek healthcare services or medications through Fridays.
  • Genetic data: E.g. where such information is included in medical history collected by Fridays.
  • Data that identifies a consumer seeking health care services: E.g. as part of the medical history collected by Fridays, or when you otherwise seek healthcare services or medications through Fridays.
  • Other information that may be used to infer, derive, or extrapolate data related to the above or other health information.

How To Exercise your MHMDA or Nevada CHD Law Rights

‍Subject to exceptions, the MHMDA and Nevada CHD Law extend certain rights relating to CHD. Depending on your jurisdiction and situation, this may include rights (1) to confirm whether Fridays is collecting, sharing or selling your CHD; (2) to access your CHD; (3) to delete your CHD; and (4) to withdraw your consent to the collection or sharing of your CHD. You can seek to exercise these rights by emailing us at privacy@joinfridays.com. Depending on the nature of your request, we may contact you for further information if appropriate to authenticate your identity.  Note, however, that Fridays will never ask you for sensitive personal or financial information when authenticating your identity, and no Fridays employee will ask you to tell them your password.If we deny your request in whole or in part and the MHMDA or Nevada CHD Law applies to our handling of your CHD, you may appeal that decision by emailing us at privacy@joinfridays.com. If you are a resident of Washington and your appeal is denied, you can contact the Washington State Attorney General at www.atg.wa.gov/file-complaint. If you are a resident of Nevada and your appeal is denied, you can contact the Nevada Attorney General at https://ag.nv.gov/Complaints/File_Complaint/.
