Common Questions

What are peptides? +
Peptides are short chains of amino acids that act as signaling molecules in your body. They tell cells to perform specific functions like healing tissues, building muscle, or regulating metabolism. Peptide therapy uses synthetic versions of these naturally-occurring compounds to enhance your body's natural processes.
Are peptides safe? +
When prescribed by a licensed physician and sourced from FDA-registered pharmacies, peptides have an excellent safety profile. Your provider will review your medical history, discuss potential side effects, and monitor your progress throughout treatment. Most patients experience minimal to no side effects.
How are peptides administered? +
Most peptides are administered via subcutaneous injection (a small needle under the skin, similar to insulin). Your provider will teach you proper injection technique, and most patients find it simple and painless. Some peptides are also available in oral or nasal spray forms.
How long until I see results? +
Timeline varies by peptide and individual goals. Some patients notice improvements within 1-2 weeks, while others may take 4-6 weeks to see full effects. Your provider will set realistic expectations based on your specific protocol and health goals.
Do I need a prescription? +
Yes, all peptide therapies require a prescription from a licensed physician. After completing your online assessment, one of our board-certified providers will review your information and determine if peptide therapy is appropriate for you.
