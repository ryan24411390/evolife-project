What is Semaglutide?

Semaglutide is a glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1) receptor agonist that mimics a naturally occurring hormone involved in appetite regulation and blood sugar control. Originally developed for type 2 diabetes, semaglutide has proven to be one of the most effective weight loss medications ever studied.

How Semaglutide Works

Semaglutide works through multiple pathways to promote weight loss:

  • Activates GLP-1 receptors in the brain to reduce appetite and food cravings
  • Slows gastric emptying to increase feelings of fullness after meals
  • Improves insulin sensitivity to stabilize blood sugar levels
  • Reduces glucagon secretion to prevent glucose spikes

Unlike restrictive diets that trigger metabolic adaptation, semaglutide addresses the hormonal imbalances that make weight loss difficult‚Äîresulting in sustainable, physician-supervised weight reduction.

Important Safety Information

Common Side Effects

Most side effects are mild to moderate and decrease over time as your body adjusts:

  • Nausea (most common, typically resolves within 2-4 weeks)
  • Diarrhea or constipation (manageable with hydration)
  • Abdominal pain or discomfort
  • Headache
  • Fatigue (temporary during adaptation)
  • Injection site reactions (redness, minor bruising)

Starting with a low dose and gradually increasing helps minimize these effects. Most patients report side effects diminish significantly after the first month.

Who Should Not Use Semaglutide

Do not use Semaglutide if you have:

  • Personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC)
  • Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2)
  • Known severe hypersensitivity to semaglutide
  • History of pancreatitis
  • Diabetic retinopathy complications

Pregnancy Warning:

Semaglutide should not be used during pregnancy or if planning to become pregnant within 2 months. Discontinue treatment at least 2 months before attempting conception. Not recommended while breastfeeding.

Your physician will review your complete medical history to determine if Semaglutide is safe and appropriate for you. Always disclose all current medications and health conditions during your assessment.

Medical Supervision Required: This medication requires ongoing physician supervision. Contact your doctor immediately if you experience severe abdominal pain, persistent vomiting, vision changes, or signs of allergic reaction. Regular monitoring ensures safe, effective treatment.
