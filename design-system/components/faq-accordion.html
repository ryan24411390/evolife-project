Everything you need to know about Semaglutide

Semaglutide is a GLP-1 receptor agonist medication originally developed for type 2 diabetes that has been proven effective for weight loss. It works by mimicking a hormone that targets areas of the brain that regulate appetite and food intake, helping you feel fuller longer and reducing cravings.

Clinical trials show that patients lose an average of 15% of their body weight over 68 weeks. Individual results vary based on factors like starting weight, diet, exercise, and adherence to the treatment plan. Most patients start seeing results within 4-8 weeks.

Common side effects include nausea, diarrhea, vomiting, constipation, and abdominal pain. These side effects are usually mild and decrease over time as your body adjusts to the medication. Your physician will start you on a low dose and gradually increase it to minimize side effects.

Serious side effects are rare but can include pancreatitis, gallbladder problems, and kidney problems. Contact your doctor immediately if you experience severe abdominal pain or persistent vomiting.

Semaglutide is approved for adults with a BMI of 30 or greater (obese), or adults with a BMI of 27 or greater (overweight) who have at least one weight-related condition such as high blood pressure, type 2 diabetes, or high cholesterol.

It's not recommended for pregnant women, people with a history of medullary thyroid carcinoma, or those with multiple endocrine neoplasia syndrome type 2. Your physician will review your medical history to determine if Semaglutide is right for you.
